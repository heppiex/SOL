SolMandalas is the first “live” algorithmically generated NFT artworks hosted on-chain on a Solana & Arweave . Each NFT will point to its own live generative algorithm - so you can watch it forever, and fall intothe depths of its calming animations!

Why did we make SolMandalas?

Everywhere you look, you see chaos. Every time you open Discord, Twitter, or even take the bus, you discover the next generative NFT project - and we believe that it’s time we found some calm in this chaos. Welcome to SolMandalas: A route to discover your own inner peace and full power of the self.

SolMandalas is a collection of 3,333 algorithmically generated animated Mandalas. Every single one of them is unique and has different properties which support the tastes of all people. SolMandalas will be brought to life through an exploration of symmetry, movement, and balance. So take a deep breath and join us as we begin our meditative journey together.

SolMandalas is a series of dynamic artworks that offer collectors an opportunity to pause, take a breath, and become more present. Created in reaction to the volatile and ever-changing landscape of NFT art - these algorithmically generative artworks undulate beautifully, inviting viewers to watch them slowly change over time, and as they become more active during the day. The collection of SolMandala NFTs are on the Solana and Arweave blockchains.

What is unique about SolMandalas?

  • Each NFT comes with its own code - permanently stored on the Arweave blockchain allowing easy access for owners.
  • The artworks respond to the time of day! During daylight hours, the SolMandalas begin to spin - becoming more active until noon - and then calming down in the evening and night time hours.
  • We believe that SolMandala NFTs are the first LIVE generative artwork to be released on the Solana blockchain.

Meet The Team

  • We are a team of people who work together in the real world.
  • SolMandalas was started by Daniel Hirschmann - a physical/digital artist who uses creative technology at the core of his work. His artwork has been exhibited at the Centre Pompidou (FR), V&A and Science Museum (UK), the Whitney Museum of American Art and National Portrait Gallery (US). His work can be found in the permanent collection of the London Design Museum and the Museum of Modern Art in NY.

The entire team and some alumni worked extremely hard to bring this project to reality - including (alphabetically): Joanne Harik, Edmund Oetgen, Miklos Onody, George Profenza, Adam Ray-Braun, Roberto Weiser, Martin White as well as @HSorta, 0xAlice, and years of learning thanks to Processing & P5JS.
